About Me

Hi! My name is Joann, and I'm from Taiwan! With a BM degree in Music Performance (Violin) from the Butler School of Music (studied under Prof. Sandy Yamamoto) at the University of Texas at Austin and a Butler Scholarship recipient from the school, I'm now working as a research assistant under supervision of Dr. Yi-Hsuan Yang at the Music and AI Lab at Academia Sinica. Whilst my stay at the lab, I've done works such as establishing a loop dataset, auto key detection, composer classification, loop role classification, beat detection, and cross-modal learning tasks.

Before joining the lab, I focused mainly on classical music performing. However, with a strong feeling that there's something more in music to discover, I've chosen to use computing techniques as the way for discovery and creation, with the use of my domain knowledge as assistance.

Until now, I'm still active in music performances, and is currently the principal of the 2nd violins at the Philharmonia Moments Musicaux in Taipei, Taiwan. Additionally, as personal interest, I maintain a blog (JJ's Music Diary) for music reviews.

Current Intrests: Coding silly games:), Algorithmic Composition & Max/MSP
